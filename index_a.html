Stress and Mental Health Q&A:


Mental Health Facts

Q: "Are all stress bad?"

A: Definitely not, we need stress to sign our bodies to prepare threats, but we will reach burnout if we are constantly in stress for a long time.

Q: "Stress has no effect on our health, right?"

A: False! chronic stress can be potentially harmful to our health.

Q: "What is chronic stress?"

A: Chronic stress occus when our bodies are used to stress overtime which we can't receive a clear signal to return back to normal.

Q: "What are the symptoms of stress?"

A: Chronic stress affect all aspects of our lives, including our emotions, behaviors, cognitvity, and physical health.

    Emotioal symptoms include:
  • - Become easily frustrated, agitated
  • - Feel overwhelmed
  • - Feel difficult to relax yourself
  • - Feel depressed (low self-esteem)
  • - Avoid interact with others

    • Behavioral symptoms include:
  • - Change in appetite
  • - Procastinate and avoiding responsibilities
  • - Increase substanse use (ex. drug, alcohol)

    • Cognitive symptoms include:
  • - Constantly worryig
  • - Racing
  • - Forgetfulness and disorganization
  • - Inability to focus
  • - Being pessimistic

    • Physical symptoms include:
  • - Low energy
  • - Headaches
  • - Aches, pains and tense muscles
  • - Rapid heartbeat
  • - Insomnia
  • - Constantly in the state of nervousness
  • - Loss sexual desire and/ or ability

Q: "Oh my goodness I am stressed out, How can I manage my stress?"

A: Don't worry, you can still change it! There are lots of way to manage stress, such as:

  • * Exercise, any exercise will boost your mood and health.
  • * Rest and eat well! Help your body before helping your mind
  • * Find a relaxing activity that suits you, for me it's listening to jazz, what about you?
  • * Stay connected with the ones who can provide emotional support and help.
  • * Structure your life, like set goals and priorties.
  • * Further browse our website, we have games and resources for you to find support!

  • If you are overwhelmed by stress, ask for help from a health professional.
